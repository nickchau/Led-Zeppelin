Here is a sample of some the Led Zeppelins most popular tracks

Good Times Bad Times

This was the first track on Led Zeppelins Debut Album "Led Zeppelin". Page, Jones and Bonham are credited as the writers of the peice which really outlined the shape of the writing process of the band. This track also marked the beginning of what would become to be known as Zeppelins signiture sounds; such as Page's use of a Leslie speaker and Bomham's fast bass drum playing and "triplet" rythem style.

Whole Lotta Love

One of the earliest song where the lyrics are written by Plany himself, Whole Lotta Love became one of the groups best love tracks. Between the blues inspired inudendo the massive drum sound created by placing the kit in a large room with mics in unusual places, and the psycidelic mid section featuring experimental production from page.This song became the only single released in the UK in 1977

Since I've Been Loving You

A seven and a half blues number was a live favourite for the band. Said to be the hardest track to record for the album, Since I've Been Loving You was a test for the entire band placing Jones on Hammond Organ and testing Plants vocal range. This song was originally meant for Led Zeppelin II but was bumped for "Whole Lotta Love". Legend tells that it only took Page a single take to master the guitar solo.

Stairway to Heaven

The most famous rock song of all time was first dreamt up by Page and Plant while hiking in the area surrounding Bron-Y-Ar. This was the only song with the lyrics printed on the inner sleeve, however it was prevented from becoming a chart hit by the bands unwillingness to release a shortened single version for radio. Once all of Zeppelins material became available for radio in finally charted in 2007

The Song Remains The Same

Based on Robert Plants belief that music is universal, The Song Remains The Same became a live favourite for the band and even became the title for their feature length film.Beginning as an instrumental called the overture Plants sped up lyrics ontop this song was used to open live shows from 1977. Jimmy Page always used his legendary double-necked guitar for this song.

Kashmir

The band was unanimous in considering this one of their greatest musical achievments. Written by plant while driving through the Sahara desert on the way to Morroco despite being far from the Northern Pakistani region of Kashmir. This song was played at every live show sicne its released and even managed to get radio credit, being played due to Stairways success.

Nobody's Fault But Mine

Inspired by blues singer Blind Willie Johnson, this song was fairly ambiguous in interpretation with many theories circling about its meaning.The song was written while Plant was recovering from a car accident and was in a wheelchair some thought this may have been the source of the meaning. The intro includes three guitars over dubs from Page.

In The Evening

Written about how the rich and famous are still suffering, this song features a drone effect used by Jimmy Page created using a Gizmotron. Recording during a period of unrest for the band when Page and Bonham were spending alot of tiem together and often showing up late to the studio and working very late. The intro is alledgedly written for a sound track for the film "Lucifer Rising"

Darlene

This song was recorded for "In Through The Out Doors" but was bumped for a lack of room. It charted at #4 despite never having being released as a single.
